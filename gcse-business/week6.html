Week 6 – Human Resources

Topics

  • Recruitment and selection
  • Training
  • Motivation
  • Organisation structures
  • Employee retention

Learn (Key Definitions)

  • Recruitment – the process of attracting applicants for a job
  • Selection – choosing the most suitable candidate for a role
  • Training – teaching employees the skills needed to do their job
  • Motivation – the willingness of employees to work hard
  • Retention – keeping staff in the business

Apply (2–4 markers)

1. State one method of recruitment a business could use (2)

2. Identify one benefit of training staff (2)

3. Explain one advantage of having motivated employees (4)

Develop (6 marker)

Explain one reason why training is important to a business.

Sentence starter: One reason training is important is... This means... This leads to... Therefore...

Master (9 marker)

Analyse the importance of motivation to a business.

Use BLT:

  • Because motivated employees may work harder...
  • This leads to higher productivity and better customer service...
  • Therefore sales, efficiency and profit may improve...

Model Answer Structure

  • Point 1: Motivation → harder working staff → higher productivity → lower costs per unit
  • Point 2: Motivation → better customer service → customer satisfaction → repeat sales
  • Conclusion: Motivation is important because it improves both efficiency and customer outcomes

Exam Tip

In HR questions, link your answer to productivity, staff retention, customer service and profit.
